Where is Chongqing?

Location Information

Chongqing is located in southwestern China at the confluence of the Yangtze and Jialing rivers. It borders the provinces of Hubei, Hunan, Guizhou, Sichuan, and Shaanxi.

Quick Facts:

  • Language: Southwestern Mandarin (Chongqing dialect)
  • Famous for: Hot pot, spicy cuisine, mountainous terrain
  • Best time to visit: Spring (March-May) and Autumn (September-November)

What is Chongqing?

The beautiful City

Chongqing is a sprawling municipality in southwest China, known for its dramatic mountainous terrain, hot pot cuisine, and the confluence of the Yangtze and Jialing Rivers.

As one of China's four direct-controlled municipalities, Chongqing has a unique cultural identity blending traditional Sichuan culture with modern urban development.

Why Visit Chongqing?

  • Experience the famous Chongqing hot pot - spicy and flavorful
  • See stunning night views from the mountainous cityscape
  • Explore the unique urban transportation including monorails through buildings
  • Visit the starting point of Yangtze River cruises to the Three Gorges
  • Discover a mix of modern skyscrapers and traditional Chinese architecture

Tips for Hotel Booking

  • Prices are higher during holidays and festivals
  • Jiefangbei and Jiangbei districts offer best locations for tourists
  • Many hotels require passport/ID for check-in

Taxi Transportation in Chongqing

Regular Taxis

Starting fare: ¥10 (first 3 km)

Additional: ¥2.0-¥2.4 per km

Yellow taxis are most common. Night rates (11pm-6am) are 20% higher.

Ride-hailing Services

Starting fare: ¥8-¥12

Additional: ¥1.5-¥2.5 per km

Didi Chuxing is the dominant app. Often cheaper than regular taxis.

Taxi Tips in Chongqing

  • Have your destination written in Chinese to show drivers
  • Taxi drivers rarely speak English
  • Rush hour (7:30-9:30am, 5-7pm) can mean heavy traffic
  • Always insist on using the meter
